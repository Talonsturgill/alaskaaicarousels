# CAROUSEL CRAFT — LinkedIn performance science

Evidence-based operating rules for the Alaska.Ai carousel machine. Compiled
2026-07 from tiered sources: [A] official LinkedIn, [B] large named studies
(van der Blom Algorithm Insights 2024/2025, Socialinsider 2026, Metricool 2026,
Buffer 2026), [C] convergent practitioner data. Full citations at bottom.
Confidence markers: (H)igh / (M)edium / (L)ow. The scorer enforces the
starred★ rules as hard gates.

## Why this format

Documents/carousels are the #1 engagement format in every 2024-2026 dataset
(Socialinsider 7.00% ER vs 6.00% video; Metricool: 17x interactions vs images;
vdB reach multiplier 1.45x). They are also the most-SAVED format, and 1 save
counts ~5x a like in reach value. Каждый swipe = dwell, and dwell is the
ranking signal (LinkedIn engineering, LiRank/LiGR/360Brew papers). Every
swipe extends dwell. We build keepable reference artifacts, not applause.

## The hard numbers

- ★ Ship a **PDF document post** (not PPTX), **one per post**, with a
  **document title** set (upload requires it). Max 100MB/300 pages; our
  target 2-25MB with a REAL VECTOR TEXT LAYER — raster text blurs under
  LinkedIn recompression; vector text survives AND feeds the semantic ranker
  (360Brew reads deck text) and accessibility mode. (H)
- ★ Canvas **1080x1350 (4:5 portrait)**. Square −20% reach, landscape −35%,
  9:16 gets cropped. Desktop renders ~552x690; mobile near full width;
  legibility is judged at ~390px wide (multiply canvas px by ~0.36). (H)
- ★ **Slide count: 8-10 total** (1 cover + 6-8 content + 1 CTA/close).
  Floor 6, ceiling 12. NEVER below 5 (−35% reach, the clearest number in the
  literature). vdB reach-optimum ~12; completion data says 6-10; dropoff
  accelerates after ~7 in weak decks — every slide must earn its place. (H)
- ★ **25-50 words per content slide.** One idea per slide. Near-empty
  decorative slides lose ~30% engagement; walls of text kill swiping. Cover
  can be fewer words; data slides count labels. (H)
- ★ Type floors at 1080px width: **body ≥32px, headlines 60-110px, hook
  display 120-170px, absolute floor 24px** (engine warns <24). Primary
  content inside the **80px safe margins** (~920x1190 zone). (M)
- Mobile-illegible text = −75% reach (vdB measured). Contrast ≥4.5:1 at the
  worst-case point under text. (H)

## Slide grammar

- **Cover = magazine cover, not slide 1 of content.** One specific claim,
  number, or named tension in ≤12 words. Huge type, single focal point, a
  swipe cue (edge-cut element beats an arrow). Strong covers ≈3x swipe-through.
  Generic covers ("AI News This Week") are the #1 documented failure. (H)
- **Slide 2 pays off immediately** — no agenda, no "let's dive in", steepest
  drop is 1→2. (M)
- **Numbered progress** ("04 / 09") in a consistent corner — completion/
  commitment effect. (M)
- **Rhythm variation**: alternate dense and breather slides; a good 9-slide
  cadence: HOOK → context → point → big-visual breather → point → data slide
  → point → synthesis → close. (M)
- **Open loops**: end slide n with unresolved tension that slide n+1 pays
  off; the single most effective swipe tactic. (M)
- ★ **Final slide = ONE ask.** Rotate: save this / debate question / follow.
  Never stack asks. Promotional content loses 60-70% reach — we are
  educational, always. The brand close carries the Alaska.Ai mark + one line.
  A source note ("sources in comments") belongs here. (H)
- **Engineered for saves**: every deck should contain at least one keepable
  element — a numbers table, a map, a checklist, a timeline. (M-H)
- Assume the PDF is downloadable and judged standalone (download is an
  official feature): no slide may depend on the caption to make sense, and
  no placeholder may ever ship. (H)

## Post copy (the caption)

- ★ **Hook completes within the first ~140 characters** (mobile fold;
  desktop ~210). No URL, no throat-clearing, declarative > question for the
  opening line. 60-70% of readers never expand. (H)
- ★ **300-800 characters total** for carousel posts (the deck carries the
  content; vdB: <500 chars optimal for carousels, −10%/extra 500). Structure:
  hook line → 1-2 context lines with natural topic keywords (the ranker
  reads text, not hashtags) → 1-2 line plain-text summary of what the deck
  covers (doubles as accessibility for screen readers, since documents have
  NO alt text) → one genuine, debatable question (+77% comments). Never
  duplicate slide text verbatim. (M-H)
- ★ **No links in the post body** (−40-60% measured; LinkedIn PM confirms
  value-mediated demotion — same outcome). Sources go in the FIRST COMMENT
  (paste block we provide), posted within ~60s. Print "sources in comments"
  on the close slide. (H)
- ★ **Exactly 3 hashtags, niche > broad, at the very end.** Hashtag following
  is dead (Oct 2024); 3-5 can slightly reduce visibility; ≥6 reads spam. Three
  satisfies discoverability hygiene without cost. (H)
- Reading level ≤ grade 8-10; >10th grade ≈ −35% reach. Short sentences. (M)
- Write at ≤ 2,900 chars only if a special edition demands it — never
  exceed LinkedIn's 3,000 hard cap. (H)

## Cadence & aftercare (for the human, printed in every email)

- Post **Tuesday-Thursday, 8-11am Alaska time** (day signal is robust;
  hour is weak — Buffer 2026 sees afternoons rising; test quarterly). (M)
- **Golden hour**: reply substantively to every comment in the first 60-90
  min; threaded author replies drive up to 2.4x reach; a dead first hour is
  rarely recovered. Post the sources comment immediately. (M-H)
- Expectations: platform-wide organic reach fell ~50% in 2025. Judge decks
  against OUR trailing median, weighting saves > comments > shares > likes. (M)
- Cadence 1/week is right; treat decks as evergreen (multi-day long-tail
  distribution since 2025). (M)

## Brand-consistency vs variety (the house resolution)

Data says same-template-every-week earns +25% reach / +35% engagement via
recognition. Our mandate says no two decks alike. Resolution — the
**constellation system**: FIXED recognition marks (Alaska.Ai wordmark,
Polaris star glyph, mono progress counter, coordinates footer, the
gold #FFC72C accent presence, dark-arctic base register) on every deck,
while composition, palette-within-family, type pairing, art system, and
narrative structure rotate hard (see DESIGN_DOCTRINE + variety dials).
Recognizable as siblings, never twins.
