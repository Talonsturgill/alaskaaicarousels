Why this, why now

Six openings, one BD calendar

CRW's careers page runs an evergreen, always-hiring posture, and this year six paid openings sat open at once across CAD, inspection, and three separate PE disciplines, at an 80-person firm. The people who write past-performance write-ups, staffing bios, and qualifications narratives for a proposal are the same people the firm is short on. This demo shows a first draft of those sections landing before anyone opens a blank document, and a compliance checklist catching a missed form before a reviewer's first read.

How it sits in your operation

One

A solicitation drops

When a state, federal, or tribal RFP or SOQ posts, a BD coordinator drops the PDF in. Nothing happens on its own before that.

Two

A draft comes back

Within the same work session, the checklist and first-pass sections are ready. No page limit or missing form gets caught only after a human already read the whole thing by hand.

Three

A human ships it

A BD coordinator or PM reviews every line, edits what needs editing, and is the only one who can call a draft ready. CRW's own staff still submit the proposal through the agency's portal, the same way they do today.

What this demo is not

  • No automated or AI-assisted submission exists or is planned anywhere in this build. A human submits every proposal, every time, through the agency's own portal.
  • The compliance checklist is a plain yes or no rule check. One AI read pulls the requirements out of the solicitation text, then a deterministic rule engine verifies them, never a second round of AI judgment.
  • No stamped engineering content, calculations, drainage design, structural design, is drafted or reviewed anywhere in this tool. That work carries the sealing engineer's own personal liability and this build never touches it.
  • The proposal and resume library shown here is illustrative. CRW's real files are not connected today, ingesting and permissioning them is the first phase of the actual build.
