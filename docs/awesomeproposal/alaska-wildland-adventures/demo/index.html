Why this, and why now

Two things Alaska Wildland Adventures already says about itself

The reservations promise

"our reservations team will respond shortly with ways to build your dream Alaska vacation... Please check your spam filter if you haven't heard from us within 48 hours."

Source alaskawildland.com/reservations/online-reservations

The Seward staff, one job posting

"Responsibilities include answering phones, taking kayak reservations, greeting guests, labeling luggage, shuttling guests and supplies, sorting backhaul items, processing garbage/recycling, and occasionally accompanying misplaced guests through Seward."

Source alaskawildland.com/employment/wp-job-openings

A small seasonal team is already answering phones, taking reservations, and doing hands-on guest logistics at the same time. The build below does not touch the phones, the luggage, or the boats. It only makes the reply-drafting part faster and more accurate.

What this tool actually does

An internal assistant, not a guest-facing chatbot

This is trained on Alaska Wildland Adventures' own package pages, lodge pages, itineraries, and policies. It helps the reservations team draft accurate guest replies faster. No guest ever talks to it. A person reads, edits, and sends every reply by hand, the same way they do today.

Two rules this tool never breaks

  • It never talks to a guest. Every draft goes to a person first, and only a person sends it.
  • It never generates a price, a date, or an availability answer. Those always come from a separate lookup table or get flagged for a person to confirm. A wrong logistics answer about a boat or flightseeing only lodge costs more than a slow one, so this build is staff-facing on purpose.

Under the hood

Three pieces, none of them magic

Their own content

Lodge pages, package pages, itineraries, and posted policies, indexed and tagged by lodge, so an answer about Skilak Lake never gets mixed up with Kenai Fjords National Park.

A separate lookup table

Price, date, and availability answers come from a dedicated table, never from the language model. If the table does not have it, the draft is flagged for a person to confirm.

A draft log

Every draft, and every edit a staffer makes to it, is logged. That log is the real accuracy record this build needs before anything guest-facing is even considered.

How this sits in your operation

Three steps, and a person on every one of them

Step one

The inquiry arrives the same way it does today

Through the web reservations form or a phone call to the Seward office. Nothing about how a guest reaches Alaska Wildland Adventures changes.

Step two

A staffer opens it in the Reply Assistant

The tool pulls the right passages from AWA's own pages and documents, tagged by lodge, and drafts a reply with a citation next to every claim. Price and availability are never generated, only looked up or flagged.

Step three

A person edits and sends it

The staffer reads the draft, corrects or adds whatever the tool missed, and sends it from their own email, exactly as they do today. No draft reaches a guest until a person has read it and pressed send.
