# Selection — 2026-07-08 — Carousel No. 1

## The pick

**Alaska's data center decision month: who says yes to AI on Alaska land,
and who is on the record.** A tightly-coupled cluster anchored by the Stak
Energy North Slope lease fight (ADL 422741), with three interlocking live
threads:

1. **Stak Energy lease (the anchor).** DNR preliminary decision to offer a
   50-year, 715.4-acre state land lease about 26 miles south of Deadhorse
   for a $10B+ gas-fired AI campus (1 to 3 GW on-site). 500+ public
   comments, fewer than a dozen in favor; deadline extended to July 17,
   4:30 p.m. Claims c01 to c29.
2. **Air Force Enhanced Use Lease.** ~4,700 acres, 12 parcels at JBER,
   Eielson, Clear for privately financed AI data centers; proposals closed
   June 29; selection now runs out of public view. Claims c30 to c44.
3. **GVEA turbine decision July 13.** A ~$120M LM6000 weighed by a Fairbanks
   co-op board while the global data center boom has pushed turbine plant
   costs up 195% since 2019 and waitlists into the early 2030s (Bloomberg
   via News-Miner). The AI boom's price contagion reaches Interior bills
   without a single local data center. Claims c45 to c54.
4. **AKLNG tax vote July 16.** The draft compromise on the 807-mile gas
   line: the gas-supply backdrop that explains why compute is being sited
   on the Slope next to stranded gas. Claims c55 to c63.

## Why this story (criteria in order)

1. **Concrete Alaska impact: maximal.** State land, state gas, federal
   parcels, co-op bills; four named deciders (DNR/the administration, the
   Air Force, GVEA's board, the Legislature) acting within 19 days.
2. **Visual potential: exceptional.** Real geography (a one-square-mile
   pad on the Dalton Highway; three bases on the Railbelt corridor; an
   807-mile line from the Slope to Cook Inlet); brutal quantities (500 vs
   fewer than 12; 1 to 3 GW vs urban Alaska's ~30%-smaller peak; 350 to
   500 MMcf/day; 7.1M cubic yards of gravel; $10B); a four-date decision
   calendar (Jun 29 / Jul 13 / Jul 16 / Jul 17).
3. **Tangibility.** Docket numbers, notice IDs, acreage, dates. Nothing
   speculative carries the deck.
4. **Send-to-a-coworker test.** The comment deadline is live for 9 more
   days when this posts; the 500-vs-12 split is the week's water-cooler
   number; "who decides" is the argument of the summer for this audience.

## The angle (position taken)

Alaska is making its biggest AI-infrastructure decisions in one July,
through four separate processes with four different deciders, and the only
one where the public is formally on the record (the Stak docket) is
running 500 to fewer-than-12 against. The frame is not "data centers bad";
it is "the decision architecture is lopsided: the money is decided in
boardrooms and evaluation committees; the public gets one docket and 9
more days." Stak's side (12 F cooling, 10% water, $10B investment, "responsible
steward") is presented fairly (c26, c27, c29); Boyle's "form letters"
dismissal (c19) is quoted against the commenters' own words (c21 to c23).
