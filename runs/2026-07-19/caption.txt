Under one road in Utqiagvik, the northernmost US city, two buried cables now teach the frozen ground to forecast its own thaw.

A Penn State team fused physics equations with machine learning into a digital twin of that embankment, fed by three winters of thermal and seismic data. This is narrow, physics-informed AI, not a general one, and it models exactly one kilometer of Alaska permafrost.

The deck walks through the buried fiber, the twin, what it actually forecasts, and the honest limit, one prototype embankment, not a statewide system.

If one lit kilometer can forecast its own thaw, should Alaska fund a decade more of that data, or spend now to rebuild the roads already breaking?
