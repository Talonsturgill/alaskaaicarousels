## DECISION: Utqiagvik permafrost digital twin (story_id: permafrost-digital-twin)

**Thesis (working):** Under one Arctic road, a pair of buried fiber-optic cables and a
physics-informed machine-learning "digital twin" now forecast what the frozen ground will
do next, a small, honest prototype for the billion-dollar problem of thawing permafrost.

**Why this story (criteria in order):**
1. Concrete Alaska impact: thawing permafrost threatens billions in Alaska roads, buildings,
   and pipelines (PERMA-12); this is a validated predictive tool on a real Utqiagvik embankment
   (PERMA-01/02/10), transferable to other cold-region infrastructure (PERMA-11).
2. Visual potential: exceptional. An "invisible nervous system" of two 1 km fiber cables
   threading a road embankment; a "digital twin" mirror/double motif (real ground vs its model);
   Utqiagvik at the top of Alaska; a subsurface cutaway; a 3-year sensing timeline.
3. Tangibility: a buried cable, a specific road, a northernmost city, dated readings.
4. Would an Alaskan send this to a coworker? Yes for DOT&PF, rural planners, engineers,
   researchers, and climate-aware civic readers. It is the rare "AI that helps Alaska" story.

**Verified support:** 12 claims (9 high-confidence), clears the >=3 bar with room for an
8-9 slide deck. Single-source (Phys.org press article; Wiley JGR paywalled) so the deck
MUST attribute clearly ("according to Penn State / the study"). Honesty firewall PERMA-11
(one prototype embankment, 3 years of data, not statewide) and the narrow-ML note PERMA-09
(physics-informed applied ML, NOT general/generative AI) are load-bearing and must appear.
Frame as recent research (~5 weeks old, published 2026-06-16), not breaking news.
