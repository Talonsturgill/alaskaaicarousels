# Phase 4 — Selection + Dedupe Gate — 2026-07-11 (Carousel No. 4)

## THE STORY (selected)

**Cook Inlet's gas ceiling: the regulator just rejected the fix.**

On July 10, 2026 the Anchorage Daily News reported that the Regulatory Commission
of Alaska denied Enstar's request for an advance determination that its proposed
Cook Inlet gas storage project was prudent, calling it an "extraordinary request"
that "lacked basic information about the project." The $240 million plan would have
bought the depleted Kenai Loop Pool reservoir from AIX Energy to hold 25 billion
cubic feet of gas (close to half Enstar's annual demand), adding up to $12 a month
to the average bill. DNR has not confirmed the pool can even hold gas, Enstar and
Hilcorp have competing lease applications for it, and Enstar president John Sims
said the company has now suspended the project.

Thesis: Alaska is being asked to power the AI boom on a gas system that cannot even
secure winter storage for the homes it already serves. STAK Energy's single proposed
North Slope AI campus could generate a gigawatt or more, some 30% more than urban
Alaska's entire peak demand (Northern Journal). The demand curve is going vertical;
the supply floor just cracked, in the same week the state's storage backstop was
rejected. The deck is about the SUPPLY-SIDE ceiling, not about who gets to comment.
