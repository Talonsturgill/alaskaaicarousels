More than 500 comments landed on a North Slope AI data center, and fewer than a dozen backed it.

The state's preliminary nod would hand Stak Energy 715.4 acres 26 miles south of Deadhorse on a 50-year lease, a gas-fired campus that could pull about 1 gigawatt, roughly 30% more than urban Alaska's whole peak grid.

The loudest objection, higher home power bills, is the weakest, because the campus makes its own power on site, off the Railbelt grid that feeds Anchorage and Fairbanks. What actually holds is quieter, 50 years of public land, about 7.1 million cubic yards of gravel on tundra, and 350 to 500 million cubic feet of gas a day. The deck grades them, loudest to strongest.

The comments ran better than 40 to 1 against, and one state agency still holds the signature. So what is that public land honestly worth to lease away for half a century?
