## SELECTED STORY (revised)
**How Alaska's tribal health system is governing AI on its own terms, and its own
data.** A peer-reviewed paper (International Journal of Circumpolar Health,
published 2026-05-23, doi 10.1080/22423982.2026.2677426) documents a 2025
Anchorage symposium and the emerging governance of AI and machine learning across
the Alaska Native tribal health system, the Alaska Native Tribal Health
Consortium (ANTHC), Southcentral Foundation, Maniilaq Association, with UAF's
Center for Alaska Native Health Research and Stanford, under Indigenous data
sovereignty principles (CARE and FAIR, Tribal approval authority over data).
Concrete Alaska stakes, air-medevac rates cited as roughly 40 times higher than
anywhere else in the US and more than 80 percent of communities without road
access, drive where clinical AI could matter (medevac decision-support, an AI
scribe already in use at a rural tribal health organization, a statewide ANTHC
AI use policy). Awareness moved fast, in 2022 about 35 percent had never heard of
AI or ML; by 2025 use had grown substantially.

## WHY THIS ONE (criteria in order)
1. **Concrete Alaska impact:** real Alaska institutions setting real rules over
   real Alaska Native health data, in a state whose geography (medevac 40x,
   80 percent roadless) makes the stakes uniquely Alaskan. This is who-decides
   and who-owns, the studio's core register.
2. **Visual potential:** strong and DIFFERENT from everything recent, a
   data-sovereignty governance framework (consent, approval authority), a roadless
   Alaska map with medevac reach, an adoption-over-time curve, the honest
   single-source evidence-tag treatment.
3. **Tangibility:** health, data, and consent are viscerally personal; "who gets
   to run AI on your medical record" lands.
4. **Send to a coworker?** Yes, for the studio's audience (tribal-corp staff,
   researchers, policy, health execs) this is a proud, substantive, non-hype read.
