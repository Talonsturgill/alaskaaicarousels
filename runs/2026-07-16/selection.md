# SELECTION + DEDUPE GATE — 2026-07-16 — Carousel No. 9

## DECISION: AIDEA's Mat-Su data-center industrial district

The state's own development bank (AIDEA) is set to receive 30 to 31 square
miles of public land north of Houston, at no cost and without competition,
to build a "multi-use industrial and energy development district" whose
named uses include scalable data centers, at the very moment the town it
borders (City of Houston) is weighing an ordinance to ban data centers.
AIDEA says "there are no pre-determined and set land use decisions." The
ground is real, given away, and enormous; the data centers are, for now,
a use listed on a plat. (Spine claims C1 to C11, verified across two
independent in-window outlets.)

### Why this story (criteria in order)
1. Strongest concrete Alaska impact: 30 to 31 sq mi of PUBLIC land, moved
   for $0, noncompetitively, to a state authority. Real ground, real money
   foregone, real local government in conflict. In-window (July 15 to 16).
2. Visual potential: a surveyed industrial DISTRICT is a built system the
   art can construct (isometric plat of ground, empty pads, rail + utility
   corridors as connective geometry) and a real place (Mat-Su, near the
   Parks Highway and the Alaska Railroad). Quantity (sq mi, $0, 8 vs 639),
   geometry (parcels/grid), place (north of Houston) all encodable.
3. Tangibility: land you can point to, a comment window a reader can use
   (through Aug 19), a town vote (Aug 13). Civic and concrete.
4. Would an Alaskan send this to a coworker? Yes. "The state is giving away
   31 square miles for free to chase data centers we barely have, and the
   town next door is trying to ban them" is a forward-to-a-friend line.

### Thesis (one sentence, distinct from No. 1)
Alaska is being asked to build the ground for a data-center boom it does
not yet have, and the first thing being spent is not power or water but
public land, handed to the state's own developer for free, purpose to be
decided later.
