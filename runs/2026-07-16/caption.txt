Alaska just moved to hand 30 to 31 square miles of public land to its own development bank for $0, with no bid and no decided purpose.

The buyer is AIDEA, the state's own development authority. The seller is the state. One government sells to itself, for free, and names the purpose, data centers or AI manufacturing, later.

The deck covers the deal, Alaska's 8 data centers against Virginia's 639, the one square mile the biggest real proposal would fill, and why the City of Houston next door is voting to ban data centers six days before the state's comment closes.

Should a state give away the ground for an industry before it decides what that ground is actually for?
