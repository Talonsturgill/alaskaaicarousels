## DECK HEADER

**Thesis.** AI tools reached Alaska classrooms before the rules did, and the fight
that matters is not whether kids use AI but who protects their data and who defines
honesty; the districts getting it right are writing the rulebook in pencil, revisable
on purpose. PDF document title: **Written in Pencil** (18 chars).

**Arc (9 beats).**
1. COVER (hook, before-after) - AI is already in class; the rulebook is still in pencil.
2. THE RECEIPT (pay) - $8,300 already spent on the tool; the policy is still a draft.
3. THE TWO QUESTIONS (frame) - who defines honesty vs who guards the data.
4. THE DATA LINE (point) - the guardrail drawn first: student data, FERPA and COPPA.
5. WHY PENCIL (HERO + breather) - write it in pencil, not ink; the rendered pencil.
6. THE PLAYBOOK (keepable data) - Alaska already wrote it: 32nd of 50, 7 principles, 6 steps.
7. NOT WHETHER, HOW (the debate) - teacher discretion vs a strict rule; the real disagreement.
8. THE DEADLINE (stakes) - fall is coming; and Kenai is not alone.
9. CLOSE (single ask) - who should define honesty in your kid's classroom.
Emotional temperature: cool/arresting (1-2) -> analytical (3-4) -> warm human peak at the
rendered pencil (5) -> steady/keepable (6) -> honest tension (7) -> urgent (8) -> resolved,
invitational (9). Palette warms from cold slate toward lit paper and a gold-sealed close.

**Slide-count rationale.** 9 slides = the save-optimal 8-10 band (CAROUSEL_CRAFT). 1 cover +
7 content + 1 close. Cadence alternates dense and breather: monument (2), argument (3-4),
rendered breather (5), keepable card (6), argument (7), escalation (8), close (9). No slide is
reorderable without breaking a loop.

**Continuity system (3 devices).**
1. MOTIF EVOLUTION - THE GRADEBOOK PAGE, WRITTEN IN PENCIL. Every slide is a ruled
   gradebook/ledger page on a dark chalkboard; a pencil writes it line by line and its STATE
   changes by SHAPE (poised -> writing -> rendered hero -> set down / eraser -> worn stub ->
   signed). Doubles as the progress read. The pencil LENGTH encodes the shrinking runway to
   the fall deadline (full at S5, worn stub at S8).
2. PALETTE ARC - "gradebook at night." Desk-lamp warmth rises from cold slate (S1-2) to fully
   lit warm paper at the keepable card (S6) to a gold-sealed close (S9): light arriving = rules
   getting written.
3. EDGE-TEASE - the ruled lines (and the pencil) run off the right edge every slide, pulling
   the swipe; the pencil tip is cut by the right edge on the cover, completing on S2.
