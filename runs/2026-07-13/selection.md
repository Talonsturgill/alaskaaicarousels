# Selection + dedupe — 2026-07-13 — Carousel No. 6

## DECISION: "The Interior's power math" (GVEA turbine vote / who pays for AI's megawatts)

On July 13, 2026 the Golden Valley Electric Association (GVEA) board votes on
whether to buy a second ~$120 million LM6000 gas turbine for its North Pole
plant. Fairbanks reporting (News-Miner) ties the spike in demand for these
turbines to large AI data centers that need firm, guaranteed power; GVEA's own
framing is general load growth. The same Interior grid (GVEA's 138 kV line) is
what the Air Force wants commercial AI data centers to interconnect to at
Eielson and Clear (Enhanced Use Lease, ~4,700 acres / 12 parcels, proposals
closed June 29), and an FY2027 NDAA provision would require a grid-impact
assessment before any such lease.

THESIS: Alaska is being asked to build and FINANCE the megawatts of the AI
buildout, on member-owned utility debt and ratepayer exposure, in a borough
already reeling from a record fuel-cost spike, BEFORE anyone has settled who is
actually on the hook, and while the biggest loads (the Air Force EULs, the
North Slope campus) remain unbuilt and unbound. The turbine vote is the
tangible pivot: a co-op deciding today to spend $120M of members' money on
firm gas capacity partly for load that is still speculative.

## WHY THIS STORY (criteria in order)
1. **Concrete Alaska impact**: a real capital vote TODAY by a member-owned
   Interior utility, with direct ratepayer consequences (long-term debt on
   ~100k Interior members) and an air-quality dimension (PM2.5 non-attainment
   borough). Perishable timeliness: the vote is today.
2. **Visual potential**: strong. Interior grid geography (Fairbanks / North
   Pole / Eielson / Clear on the 138 kV corridor); a MW capacity ladder
   (existing units vs the proposed turbine vs the loads it would serve); a
   who-pays flow diagram (members -> turbine -> speculative AI load); a
   decision-scale contrast (the $120M vote vs the still-unbound EUL/North Slope
   loads). Numbers everywhere.
3. **Tangibility**: a turbine, a power bill, a co-op board room. Not abstract.
4. **Send-to-a-coworker**: yes. Every GVEA member, every Railbelt ratepayer,
   every utility/policy professional in the state has a stake in "who pays for
   AI's power," and it is being decided this week.
