# Selection — 2026-07-12 — Carousel No. 5

## DECISION: wildfire-robots
**Alaska is the world's proving ground for autonomous wildfire-fighting robots.**

The finals of XPRIZE Wildfire's Autonomous Response track ($11M, four-year global
contest) are being field-tested on a 1,000 square kilometer range in Alaska, run
through the University of Alaska Fairbanks' ACUASI (an FAA-designated UAS test
site). Five finalist teams (Anduril, Data Blanket, Dryad, FireSwarm Solutions,
Wildfire Quest) are competing to prove machines can detect and put out a fire
before a human arrives. The winner is expected in September 2026. One finalist,
Dryad, says it detected and autonomously suppressed a fire with no human on scene
during the Alaska trials (attributed, not independently verified).

### Why this story (criteria in order)
1. **Concrete Alaska impact**: Alaska is not a backdrop here, it is the arena.
   ACUASI/UAF runs the test range; the state's roadless vastness and fire record
   (3 of its 4 largest fire seasons in 25 years; ~half of all US acreage burned in
   the 2022 example year) are the REASON it was chosen. The stakes are Interior
   communities and Native villages that crews cannot reach in minutes.
2. **Visual potential**: exceptional. The autonomy loop (detect > decide >
   dispatch > douse), a sensor mesh over dark Alaska with an ignition point, the
   1,000 km2 zone scaled against a familiar reference, a five-team finalist field,
   a rendered hero of a machine over a fire-lit interior. Geometry + quantity +
   place all drawable.
3. **Tangibility**: robots putting out fires, autonomously, right now, here.
4. **Send-to-a-coworker**: yes. It is surprising ("Alaska is the test range for
   the world's firefighting robots"), civic, and swipeable.

## Directors-room synthesis

Three lenses: field-documentarian ("Field Range"), cinematographer ("First
Machine to the Fire"), systems-illustrator ("Five Robots, One Fire"). All three
converged on the STORY spine (single machine racing a fire in a vast Alaska
dusk; "Dryad says" honesty; "Save this for September" close; Archivo + JetBrains
Mono; cold-dusk palette with a small warm ignition). They diverged on the
STRUCTURAL idea, and the synthesis grafts the best organ of each.

WINNER STRUCTURE: the systems-illustrator's **closing Autonomy Ring** as the
primary continuity motif. The detect/decide/dispatch/douse loop is drawn as a
control-system seal with a GAP at the top; it gains one lit arc per slide and
seals with a gold Polaris at the September verdict. It is the freshest new
structural idea (design_variance 4), it doubles as the progress counter, it
encodes the thesis (can the loop close with no human?), and it resolves the
cinematographer's own flagged risk that a camera-move "closing gap" is invisible
to a fast scroller. Distinct from all four forbidden heroes (map-camera,
glyph-ledger, trace-conductor instrument panel, subsurface cutaway spine): it is
a discrete cyclic STATE machine, not a continuous trace bleeding off edges.

GRAFTED HERO: the cinematographer's de-risked **single-actor deep-field rendered
machine** (akthree GPU PBR, backlit silhouette so there is no lumpy-drone slop
risk, render budget on atmosphere + one key light; AK3D multiplane fallback).
Lands at the breather (S6), not the cover, so a render failure degrades
gracefully without wrecking the first impression. Camera math from the
cinematographer (fov ~52, low monumental horizon planned via horizonY).

GRAFTED DATA: the systems-illustrator's honest parallel-projection data slides
(the ~300 to 5 finalist funnel; the purse bars) and the field-documentarian's
reportage restraint (weather/place dominate, machine stays small).

HONESTY RESOLUTION (all three flagged the tension): the emotional climax is the
VERDICT slide (the open September question + the Alaska.Ai position that the
machines are still auditioning and the real stakes are Interior communities crews
cannot reach in minutes), NOT the Dryad demo. Dryad's self-report sits mid-deck
(S6) as one attributed data point ("Dryad says"), never the payoff. Drama and
honesty now point the same way.

FINAL: title "First Machine to the Fire"; 9 slides; hero = closing Autonomy Ring
+ rendered dusk machine; devices = ring-motif-evolution + palette-arc; palette =
spruce-night cold with a small fire-orange antagonist and cyan/mint machine cue;
type = Archivo variable + JetBrains Mono; dials 4/3/2. See storyboard.md.
