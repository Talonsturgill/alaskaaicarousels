Five teams are racing across 1,000 square kilometers of Alaska to prove a machine can fight a wildfire with no human in the loop.

This is the XPRIZE Wildfire autonomous track, an 11 million dollar contest field-tested through the University of Alaska Fairbanks. The winner is expected in September 2026.

The deck covers the Alaska test range, the five finalists cut from nearly 300 teams, the loop each machine has to close from spark to suppression, the prize money, and the caveat that keeps getting skipped: a contest winner is not the same as proof.

Would you trust an autonomous machine to reach an Interior fire before a crew could, or does that claim need a full Alaska summer first?
